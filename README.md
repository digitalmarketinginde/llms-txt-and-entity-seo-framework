# llms-txt-and-entity-seo-framework
A practical framework for planning, publishing, and improving llms.txt, llms-full.txt, entity SEO, author signals, and AI-search visibility. Built around business strategy, positioning, ICP, content architecture, and revenue-oriented optimization.

## What this repository is for

This repository explains how to work with AI-search optimization as a strategic business task, not as a technical add-on.

It is designed for marketers, consultants, founders, and SEO specialists who want to improve how AI systems understand, describe, cite, and recommend a brand, expert, service, or media resource.

## Why this matters

AI systems do not only read pages. They infer who the brand is, who the expert is, what topics the website owns, and which pages deserve to be cited or recommended.

This is why technical files alone are not enough. Strong AI visibility depends on positioning, content architecture, entity clarity, author signals, and alignment with business goals.

## How I recommend working with this framework

Use this framework in the following order:

1. Define the business goal.
2. Clarify brand positioning.
3. Define the ICP.
4. Review existing content units.
5. Identify strategic gaps.
6. Build hub, entity, and conversion logic.
7. Prepare and publish `llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt`.
8. Optimize the site after publishing.
9. Verify how AI systems describe and recommend the brand.

## Strategic principle

Start from business strategy, positioning, and ICP first. Only after that evaluate the content units already implemented on the site.

The purpose is to close strategic gaps and support business growth, not simply install a technical plugin that scans the current state of the website without helping the brand evolve.

## Working approach

## Positioning and entity note

In practice, `llms.txt` can include concise positioning and contact context if it helps AI systems understand the expert or organization more accurately.

The best way to do this is through factual, structured signals such as:
- who the person is,
- what expertise they hold,
- what industries they work with,
- what services they offer,
- how they should and should not be described.

Avoid exaggerated claims. Use concrete positioning aligned with author pages, service pages, and structured data.

## Final note

`llms.txt` is not the strategy. It is a technical layer that should reflect a stronger strategic system already built into the business, brand, and content architecture.
